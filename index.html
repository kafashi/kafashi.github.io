Where complexity becomes geometry.

Holography says a strongly-coupled boundary theory can conjure an entire bulk spacetime out of nothing but its own correlations. My work asks what quantum complexity — the resources it takes to prepare a state — has to do with that: whether the depth of the bulk is really tracking how hard the boundary theory is working, and whether gravity itself is a byproduct of that difficulty.
