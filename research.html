Papers

Krylov Complexity for Time-Dependent Generators

2026

Growth of Krylov complexity in quantum systems with time-dependent Hamiltonians, with approximation methods for periodically driven and general non-periodic dynamics when exact solutions aren't available.

arXiv:2607.10454 ↗

Local Renormalization Group as Quantum Complexity

2025

Graduate thesis, supervised by Dr. A. F. Astaneh. Introduces a notion of quantum complexity built on the local renormalization group, general across dimensions and field theories, and shows its compatibility with path-integral complexity in two dimensions. Complexity and optimized spacetime geometry are worked out for free and self-interacting scalar fields.

Thesis — write-up in preparation

Quantum-Compatible Approach to Complex System Dynamics

2025

With Dr. M. R. Rahimi Tabar, Dr. A. Rezakhani, and collaborators. A quantum-information–inspired framework for multivariate time series, encoding system states as density matrices and using fidelity and entropy to quantify interdependence and recovery timescales — validated on a 9-dimensional Lorenz-96 model and applied to global temperature anomaly data.

arXiv:2512.14169 ↗

Fast Radio Bursts as Cosmic Lightning

2025

Supervised by Dr. S. Rahvar. Models FRBs as sudden discharges of accumulated charge in the accretion disks of compact objects, with Compton scattering forming a capacitor-like system stabilized by radiation pressure. Predictions are compared against observational data (FRB20180725A).

arXiv:2508.03620 ↗

Exploratory research

2024 – 2025

Krylov Complexity in Curved Spacetime and Fast Scrambling

Formulated a modified Ising Hamiltonian encoding geometric effects to study Krylov complexity in curved backgrounds, using Lanczos-coefficient growth as a probe of scrambling. This project seeded the local-RG complexity idea above.

2024 – 2025

Large Language Models as O(N)

Collaborated with Dr. Haghighat and his student at Tsinghua University on reformulating Transformer architectures as O(N) models, estimating internal model dimension and connecting scaling behavior and phase transitions to the Ising model.

2023

Drag Force in AdS/CFT

Extended Gubser's work on drag forces in N=4 Super-Yang-Mills plasma via AdS/CFT and classical string approximations, studying how extrinsic geometry affects the drag on an external quark.
